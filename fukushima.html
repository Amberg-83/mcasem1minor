FUKUSHIMA DAIICHI — 2011

Overview & Context

On 11 March 2011 a magnitude 9.0 earthquake caused a massive tsunami that struck Japan’s east coast...

Flooding disabled backup generators and cooling systems, leading to core meltdowns in multiple reactors.

It remains one of the costliest and most complex nuclear cleanups in history.

Timeline

11 Mar 2011

Tsunami hits; cooling lost.

12–15 Mar 2011

Hydrogen explosions and radiation releases.

2011–Now

Large-scale cleanup, water treatment, long-term decommissioning.
