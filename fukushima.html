Chernobyl Nuclear Disaster (1986)

Overview

The Chernobyl disaster occurred on April 26, 1986, at the Chernobyl Nuclear Power Plant in Ukraine (then part of the Soviet Union). A late-night safety test went wrong, resulting in a steam explosion and fire that released massive amounts of radioactive material into the atmosphere. It is considered the worst nuclear accident in history.

The explosion destroyed Reactor 4 completely, scattering radioactive graphite and fuel particles across the region. Emergency workers, firefighters, and plant staff were exposed to extremely high radiation levels while attempting to control the fire and prevent further explosions. The radioactive plume spread across Europe, contaminating land for thousands of kilometers.

Long-term effects included cancer cases, genetic disorders, wildlife mutations, and the creation of the 2,600 kmÂ² Exclusion Zone. The disaster highlighted major flaws in reactor design, safety culture, and emergency preparedness. Today, Chernobyl remains a symbol of the catastrophic risks associated with nuclear power mismanagement.
