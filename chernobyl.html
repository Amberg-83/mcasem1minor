Overview & Context

The Chernobyl disaster occurred on April 26, 1986, due to a flawed reactor design and serious operator errors during a safety test, causing a catastrophic explosion.

Causes of the Disaster

The accident resulted from human error combined with an unstable RBMK reactor design. Operators disabled safety mechanisms, triggering an uncontrollable power surge.

Technical Failure Mechanism

Excessive reactor power caused fuel rupture and a steam explosion. The exposed graphite moderator ignited, releasing radioactive particles directly into the atmosphere.

Short-Term and Long-Term Impacts

Immediate impacts included severe radiation exposure and evacuation. Long-term effects include increased cancer rates, environmental contamination, and permanent exclusion zones.

Lessons Learned

The disaster highlighted the importance of strong safety culture, proper training, and independent regulatory oversight in nuclear power operations.
