Chernobyl Nuclear Disaster (1986)

The Chernobyl disaster occurred on 26 April 1986 in Reactor 4 of the Chernobyl Nuclear Power Plant in Pripyat, Ukraine. A failed safety experiment caused a steam explosion, releasing enormous amounts of radiation.

Cause:

Reactor design flaw + operator errors during safety test.

Deaths:

About 31 immediate deaths; long-term deaths estimated between 4,000–30,000.

Pollutants Released:

  • Iodine-131
  • Cesium-137
  • Strontium-90
  • Xenon-133
