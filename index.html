Major Nuclear Disasters – Case Study Project

Nuclear disasters remain some of the most devastating events in human history. They serve as powerful reminders of how technological failure, natural disasters, or human error can lead to large-scale environmental contamination and long-term health impacts. This project presents case studies of two of the most significant nuclear accidents: the **Chernobyl Disaster (1986)** in the Soviet Union and the **Fukushima Daiichi Disaster (2011)** in Japan.

Both disasters occurred under very different circumstances—Chernobyl due to flawed reactor design and a failed safety test, and Fukushima due to a massive earthquake followed by a tsunami. Yet, both resulted in widespread radioactive contamination that affected land, water, animals, and human settlements for decades. This project compares these incidents and simulates the radioactive plume spread using Python and Folium.

Explore each disaster page to view images, heatmap simulations, causes, impacts, pollutants released, and historical background. These case studies remain essential for understanding nuclear safety and improving future reactor technologies.
