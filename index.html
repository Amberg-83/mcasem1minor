Major Nuclear Accidents: Overview

Nuclear accidents are catastrophic failures of nuclear reactors, resulting in the release of radioactive materials into the environment. Two of the most significant disasters in human history are the Chernobyl Disaster (1986) and the Fukushima Daiichi Accident (2011).

Chernobyl Nuclear Disaster – 1986

The Chernobyl disaster occurred in Pripyat, Ukraine due to a failed safety test that caused a massive explosion in Reactor 4. It released radioactive material across Europe and remains the worst nuclear accident in history.

Fukushima Daiichi Nuclear Accident – 2011

Triggered by a massive earthquake and tsunami, the Fukushima Daiichi power plant suffered reactor meltdowns and hydrogen explosions, releasing radioactive material into the atmosphere.
